I copped the Adidas Nizza on sale from Zappos for $40. Retailing from Adidas for $65, this shoe is not part of their skateboarding line. Sporting vulcanized construction, canvas uppers and a rubber toe cap, the Nizza is a stylish shoe that skates well. Unforunately, not being part of their skateboarding line, the Nizza has serious issues on a board. The canvas uppers will need to be immediately shoe-gooed if you'd like them to last. The metal eyelets sit low and are not recessed - I was going through a lace every session. Shoe goo can help save the laces as well, but it's a real annoyance.

The insoles that come standard in this shoe are thin and have some arch padding that did not agree with my flat feet. It turned my foot slightly towards the pinky toe which caused ankle pain after several sessions. I ended up putting some Vans Ultracush insoles in which flattened the platform and provided some much-needed impract protection. After solving that issue, the shoe was much more comfortable. I am a big fan of rubber toe caps - both how they look and how they flick. The toe is a bit asymmetrical, which can take some getting used to. Overall, this shoe skates really well if you can replace the insoles and don't mind putting glue on a brand new shoe. I would not pay $65 for this with the intention of skating it, but I would definitely buy it again if I find it on sale.
