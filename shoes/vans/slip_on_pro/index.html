Vans Slip On Pro

The Vans Slip On Pro is a classic skate slipper, and at $60 it represents one of the cheaper entries in Vans' Pro model line. The Duracap toe provides that Vans flick we all love while also enhancing the durability and grip of the toe. Vans Ultracush insoles offer decent impact protection in the heel and arch area without heavily impacting the board feel you would expect in a slipper.

Sporting thin suede uppers and a low cut, this slipper does not offer much protection from rogue skateboards. The boardfeel and small footprint make this a worthy tradeoff for many skaters. The shoe does fit tightly out of the box. It's best to size down a little as the suede will stretch, and without laces, there is no way to precisely adjust the fit. Additionally, the stitching on the inside of the upper can annoyingly rub the top of the foot. However, once broken in the Slip On Pro is very comfortable.

I've skated this shoe many times and have learned the hard way to immediately apply shoe goo around the elastic area. If left alone, the elastic is prone to ripping and once it goes, the shoe will fit loosely. If you attempt to repair it after the fact, it can fit too tightly.

I would only order this shoe in an all-suede colorway. The white and checkerboard models have canvas uppers which are not as durable. There is a thin rubber reinforcement beneath the canvas but it did not last and the shoe quickly exploded.

In conclusion, the Slip On Pro is a great shoe for the price if in a suede colorway. Once broken in and glued around the elastic, this shoe feels great and I usually skate through the soles before the upper goes. This is one of my go-to shoes and it has an amazing flick.
