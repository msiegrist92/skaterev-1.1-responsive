Vans Sk8 Hi Pro

The Sk8 Hi is Vans' flagship high top model, and one of their longest running shoes. At $70, it sits at an average price across Vans' Pro line of shoes. Standard issue Ultracush insoles offer some impact protection without spoiling boardfeel. The suede uppers offer great durability. I did rip stitching by the laces, but this hasn't been a continuous issue. The panel on the sides is nicely placed to protect the shoe from ollies.

One of my favorite things about this shoe is that it does not feel or skate like a big hightop. The upper is light and flexible enough that it doesn't feel intrusive. I was instantly feeling comfortable skating these after switching from a slipper. The shoe is quite comfortable and the only issue I had was the tongue shifting around rubbing the top of my feet.

The Duracap toe from Vans is grippy and flicks very well. Vans gives a nice consistent flick as the toes of a lot of their shoes are shaped similarly. I've had no long-term durability issues with this shoe, and no real need for shoe goo. Overall, for $70 this is a home run from Vans and definitely a shoe worth having.
