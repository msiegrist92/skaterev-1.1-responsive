The timeless Chuck Taylor could be a good skate shoe if it weren't it's shortcomings. The shoe features all canvas uppers, a low cut and very thin soles. The Chuck Taylor is notorious for foot pain and rolled ankles. Skating stairs and landing primo in these is a nightmare. The canvas uppers explode as expected, but nothing some shoe goo can't fix. The positives here are amazing board feel, and a great flick from the rubber toe cap. The thin, grippy toe can take some adjusting to, but it is well worth it. With a thin footprint, skaters with wider feet may feel cramped. If you're into manuals and flatground, and don't mind gluing shoes it could be worth a try.
