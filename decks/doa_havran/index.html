Dead Or Alive

}

Beware

8.1 x 31.75 x 14.25

Tail : 6.4"

Tapered

Nose : 6.75'

Tapered

Wanted

I set this deck up on my standard 1/8" risers, 55mm Formula 4 Classics(probably about 52 now), and Indy 139s. This deck has a flat concave which always pairs nicely with Indys. The deck is just a hair wider than 8.1" advertised. The nose has a bit of a taper to it - the kick doesn't begin to round out for ~3". There's about three fingers worth of space on both the nose and tail before the kicks begin to rise up. This space makes the deck feel pretty cozy in a wider, transition stance, despite its shorter length and smaller kicks. At 31.75, the deck is a bit on the shorter side. A lot of decks between 8" and 8.25" will be a full 32" in length. However, I think there's a trend currently towards more compact decks, and the several other DOA boards I've had have also fit this bill.

The shorter length makes the board really nice to flip around but a shorter platform will be more unstable. I found the board felt a bit squirrely at times, but in a fun oh-shit-we-surfin' way. DOA's boards are made by South Central in Alabama. Although not one of the big powerhouse woodshops of the industry, the quality of this board is on par with the major shops. I've skated this board for around a month and it still feels stiff and snappy. Overall, I think this is a great all-arounder at 8.1. The compact shape is great for flip tricks, but the shorter length and wheelbase might not be the best for speed freaks and transition skaters. I really liked this board for scoopy tricks, manuals and general street-style cruising.
