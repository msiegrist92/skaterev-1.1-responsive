Real's Heavyweight series of boards features eight plys instead of the traditional seven and according to their website they're "12.5% stronger" and "hold[] that new board pop, feel and stiffness longer." I've been on this board for 6 total weeks and it still feels very fresh and new.

Despite having an extra ply, and "thicker cross grain x-bands" the deck doesn't feel heavier than other similarly sized boards. I didn't have issues with my pop or session length. The tail has a pretty standard popsicle shape, whereas the nose is a bit more square. The kicks are big and cozy, and combined with the 32" length and 14 3/8 wheelbase, the board is stable and just tons of fun to ride around.

Coming from smaller dimension boards, there were more pronounced differences in tricks I preferred on this board. First off, I loved the shape of the nose. Nollie tricks felt snappy and there's plenty of room to land on a nose manual - one of my favorite tricks. Kickflips and fakie flips felt amazing on this board - the larger length and wheelbase really aid in getting a nice rotation.

I did have issues getting my scooping tricks all the way around and finding the manual balance on this deck was a bit tricky. My go-to front shuvs required more oomph to get fully rotated, but I was rewarded with a nice pop whenever I could muster it around.

Overall, this is one of my favorite boards that I've skated. I'd definitely buy another one, as it does seem to retain that fresh-feel longer. Any shortcomings in tricks are just a matter of personal preference and adjusting to what you're riding. It may be a bit big for technical street skating to some but I found the extra effort a worthwhile investment.
